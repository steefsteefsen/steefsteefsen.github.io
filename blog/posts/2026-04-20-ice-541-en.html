ICE 541, from little-b to big-B

€90 ticket. €11.50 coffee and a roll. Free of charge: getting barked at in the dining car by someone in the worst possible mood. Nothing personal — that's the symptom, not the cause.

Do I, SteefSteefsen, have to do an apprenticeship in Japan and then buy Deutsche Bahn for this to actually work?

Paula Herber taught me at university how to build state machines properly. Ever since, when I look at the Bahn, I see nothing but question marks.

After the VW disaster — including a contract breach mid-COVID — I barely drive anything but Flix now. But when the schedule is genuinely dense, I need the Bahn. And then one very simple principle applies:

Service means SERVICE.

Dear Deutsche Bahn, dear German industry: stop resting on inherited advantages. Other countries are working — while we whine about staying on time.
