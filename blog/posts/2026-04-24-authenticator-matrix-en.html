The Three Guardians of 2FA

On March 19, 2024, Twilio shut down the Authy desktop apps1. Three months later, 33 million Authy phone numbers landed on ShinyHunters' table2. And LastPass is still collecting fines from the 2022 breach — most recently £1,228,283 from the UK ICO in November 20253, plus a $24.45 M class-action settlement (initial approval 2025)4.

Correction (April 24, 2026): I had listed FreeOTP+ as "stale" — that's wrong. Release 3.2 shipped on March 24, 2026 and the latest commits are from April 12, 20265. FreeOTP+ is actively maintained; it remains Android-only and with narrower import support than Aegis, but it's not dead. Thanks for the catch.

Time for a spring-clean of the 2FA shelf. I compared 22 authenticator apps — ranked by reliability, security, privacy, and respect for the user, in that order. Three survive.

The three guardians

Ente Auth — open source (AGPLv3), end-to-end encrypted, with native clients on Linux, Windows, macOS, Android, iOS and in the browser6. Audited twice by Cure53: Feb/Mar 20237 and October 2025, CERN-funded8. Listed prominently on the Privacy Guides MFA page9. No ads, no forced account, full import/export.

Bitwarden Authenticator — GPL-3, mobile only (iOS + Android)10. The perfect companion to my self-hosted Vaultwarden on the Pi 5, as long as you keep the "Allow authenticator syncing" toggle11 strictly off. Then your password and your second factor live on different devices — and 2FA means 2FA again.

KeePassXC + KeePassDX — for the purists. A second .kdbx file, TOTP only, with its own master password, synced via Nextcloud/WebDAV on the Pi. Zero subscription, zero vendor, full control. Current stable: 2.7.11, released November 23, 202512. KeePassXC recommends the separate-DB setup in its own FAQ13. On iOS, pair with KeePassium (Cure53-audited October 2024)14.

No authenticator without open source, a real export, and a clear path out of the vendor's cloud.

Four that get kicked out

  • Authy — desktop dead, 33 M numbers leaked, no real export.
  • LastPass Authenticator — the trust damage keeps compounding.
  • Raivo OTP — sold to Mobime in July 2023, announced via Twitter/X15; Privacy Guides removed it in v3.14 on July 27, 202316.
  • Tofu (iOS) — last App Store release v1.11 in October 2021; app-lock issue open since 201917. A few security commits on GitHub in December 2023 and June 2024, but not meaningfully alive.

For high-value accounts

For the crown jewels — email root, Bitwarden itself, banking — the seed belongs on hardware, not in an app. Enroll two YubiKey 5.7+ (or Nitrokey 3, open-source firmware on Rust/Trussed)18 simultaneously at setup. Firmware 5.7 raised OATH credential slots to 64 (up from 32)19. The Yubico Authenticator has clean Linux packages; the secrets never leave the chip.

An important note on the EUCLEAK side-channel (NinjaLab, September 3, 2024)20: the attack targets Infineon's SLE78 crypto library and affects YubiKeys with firmware before 5.7. 5.7 switched newly produced units to Yubico's own crypto library — existing older keys cannot be retrofitted in firmware, because the flaw sits in silicon21.

And one rule I've written into my own playbook: the TOTP seed for Bitwarden never goes into Bitwarden. Same way the Nextcloud recovery key doesn't live on the device that is the Nextcloud.
