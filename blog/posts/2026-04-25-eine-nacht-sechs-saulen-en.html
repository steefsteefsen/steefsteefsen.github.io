One night, six pillars

The site grew tonight. Not in the way you plan on a roadmap — in the way that happens when at 4am you stop asking permission.

Six new pillars now stand: Philosophy, Science, Ethics, Mentorship, Tribe, Movement. Each with its own voice. None finished. All here.

What changed

Philosophy — the line under everything: three concentric circles (person, community, system), six non-negotiables. One line at the bottom: Build things that make the people who use them more free, not less.

Science — no more hide-and-seek. I trust science 100%. Split into behavioural and depth psychology, because both need each other. Pavlov without Jung becomes marketing. Jung without Pavlov becomes esoterica.

Ethics — blueprint, in motion. Four pillars: gentleman/lady reframed, awareness as a posture, drug checking as realism, the right of every person to call a sexual assault by its name. With "Watch your language." as the central quote — because language is behaviour.

Mentorship — what it looks like when I mentor someone. Four first PRs, no curriculum, no certificate. Open to anyone who shares the values.

Tribe — the European Tribe Network. A Matrix room, not a Discord. Anti-spectacle, not anti-anything. Children in the room — that changes how all of us speak.

Movement — the Movement of the Working. Not an electoral party. A political demand. Launch on May 1, 2026, Workers' Day. In the tradition of the good Pirates — without repeating their mistakes. Whoever works should also get to decide.

How this happened

It grew out of a conversation about Timo, a student I'm mentoring. The conversation became an onboarding doc. The doc became a question: shouldn't everyone have this? The question became: yes, everything out of hiding, everything on the site, everything open.

Radically open. That was the word. And once it was said, there was no going back — the docs/ hideouts became public pillars, philosophy moved into the main menu, the movement got a date.

Six pillars in one night is a lot. It is not "finished." It is here. And "here" is worth more than "finished," because "here" is what lets people respond — and "finished" is something an idea should never honestly claim about itself.

Soundtrack

Three nights of nightshift. A second person working in parallel, no one watching. The track writes itself.

Tomorrow, May 1 is one day closer. Until then the pillars keep growing. If something is missing on any of them: write me. This site is not mine. It is here because it belongs shared.
