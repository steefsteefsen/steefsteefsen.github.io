Mirror live — everything at once

One mirror commit brought the whole site to GitHub. Not because I have a release process — because GitLab mirrors automatically on push. What ended up inside it was more than I originally planned.

Six new subpages went live: Ethics, Philosophy, Science, Tribe (European Tribe Network), Mentorship, and Roadmap. The Ethics page is still marked as a blueprint — structure is there, content is growing. The Tribe page describes a network for people in Europe tired of "paying attention to noise" being mistaken for "caring about the world."

The blog now has proper infrastructure: RSS feeds in German and English, Giscus comments (GitHub login, no trackers), and an automated daily-post agent that writes exactly this — every day at 10am, when something shipped. The source is open under scripts/daily-post.js.

No shipping day without public proof.

Along the way, the icons in the "Built With" section on the homepage were brought up to date: Bitwarden, Claude, GitLab, Firefox, Ollama, Mixxx, PyCharm, Python, Linux Mint, Raspberry Pi, Thunderbird, Ubuntu, Wu-Tang, and South Park now have SVG artwork instead of placeholders. What's next: filling in the Ethics page, linking the Tribe page to actual meetup spots, and moving the mentorship materials for Timo into real pull requests.
