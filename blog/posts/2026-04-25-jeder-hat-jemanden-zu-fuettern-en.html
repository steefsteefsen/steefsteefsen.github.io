April 25, 2026

Everyone has someone to feed

Short and clear. A call, now — to anyone who will be in the streets in the coming days. Police and activists. Left and right. Inside and outside.

Stay peaceful. Stay respectful.

On both sides of every line stands a human being. With a family. With debt. With a child who wants bread in the morning. With a mother who picks up the phone when the call comes at 10 p.m. With a dog at the door, tail wagging.

Everyone has someone to feed.

That isn't a slogan. That's the condition under which we face each other. The officer in the helmet isn't only a helmet. The activist with the sign isn't only a sign. Both are going home tonight. Both will be asked how their day was. Both have someone hoping they come home whole.

What respectful means

Respectful doesn't mean agreeing. Doesn't mean silent. Doesn't mean yielding.

Respectful means: leaving the other person their face. No escalation for escalation's sake. No trophies. No humiliation that lives in someone's head ten years from now.

Whoever has the better argument doesn't need to shout. Whoever enforces the law doesn't need to kick. Strength that has to bend down was never strength.

To the police

You have a duty. You also have a choice in how you carry it out. Proportionality isn't a bureaucratic word — it's the difference between a shift and a wound someone carries for life. And you have someone at home. Get home whole.

To the activists

Your cause matters. Otherwise you wouldn't be there. But a cause doesn't get stronger because a stone flies — it gets stronger because an argument lands. The images made today travel tomorrow. Make images that speak for you, not against you. And you have someone at home. Get home whole.

To everyone else

If you see it, say it. Stay peaceful. Stay respectful. Three words, in a charged moment, can shift more than a tweet afterward.

Peace cannot be kept by force; it can only be achieved by understanding.
— Albert Einstein

If you want to see where escalation leads

If you don't yet know what happens when proportionality is lost, watch Hamburger Gitter — the documentary by the leftvision collective about the 2017 G20 protests in Hamburg. 78 minutes that make visible what happens when freedom of assembly and freedom of the press become bargaining chips. Not a partisan film. A record of evidence.

Get home whole. All of you. Tonight.
