Calling things what they are

Two things from yesterday with the same root.

A new post: "Calling a genocide a genocide" — a legal stocktake on three ongoing cases. The argument: German § 130 makes denying past genocides criminal; the same legal order obligates states, under the UN Genocide Convention, to prevent and punish present ones. Both presuppose the term is actually used — not circumscribed, not replaced with "alleged atrocities". Thirteen footnotes, all primary sources.

Running alongside: an audit of all German-language text across the site. Generic-masculine forms out, neutrum-first in. Nutzer → Nutzende. Aktivisten → Aktive. Der kleine Mann → das einfache Leben. Affected: the homepage, i18n.js, the Movement, Philosophy, and Science pillar pages, several existing blog posts. The rule is now codified in CLAUDE.md with a five-step hierarchy — neutrum noun first, then substantivised participle, collective form, wer … verb construction, colon as last resort — and grep patterns for catching regressions.

Both changes come from the same place: language as a tool for precision, not cover.
