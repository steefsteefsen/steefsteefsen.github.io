Things take time

I live with ADHD. That isn't a surprise to anyone who reads this site for a while — the jumps, the connections that cut across everything, the pace. What was less clear for a long time, to me and to the people around me: that the pace is both a gift and a burden, and that not everyone carries it the same way. And that's fine.

There is a saying I have heard often in many African contexts: "Europeans have the watches — we have the time." The exact origin is contested,1 but the point survives every attribution. It isn't about being slow. It is about being present.

Two recommendations:

Hummelhirn — Judith Holofernes

The new book by the former singer of Wir sind Helden, out 12 March 2026 with Kiepenheuer & Witsch.2 Holofernes describes herself as someone with "plenty of attention but no working filter to sort the impressions" — ADHD, in plain words. The audiobook is read by Nora Tschirner, and the two are touring together with the book.3 It is the kind of book I want to put in the hands of people who work or live with me — so they understand what the inside of this looks like.

Relax — Sido

When I need to come down, music does more than any plan. One track that does it reliably is Relax by Sido — first released on Aggro Ansage Nr. 2X (2006), later on #Beste (2012).4 The voice that always has to be too fast listens for three minutes. That is often enough to put the day back in order.

What this has to do with Elon

A few weeks ago I published an Open Letter to Elon Musk. The letter was sharp because the situation was sharp. Since then Elon has responded — and invested in Mistral, i.e. in European open-source AI. That changes the tone, not the line. The letter received an update today, transparently appended: Pluralism leads to futurism. You took the first step.

Elon lives publicly with ADHD himself. Anyone carrying the kind of pace he carries knows what is meant here. Things take time. Even when you own the watch — the time isn't automatically yours.

Not everyone is as fast as us. And we ourselves are not always on time like the Japanese train system.

Make tea. Read Hummelhirn when it comes out. Listen to Relax when it gets too much. Write your letters. Update them when something shifts. Stay with it.
