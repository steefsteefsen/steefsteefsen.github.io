29 April 2026

one css file instead of thirty-six

The site's colour palette lived as an inline :root block in every HTML file — thirty-six copies. Changing --clay meant running find-replace across half a dozen subdirectories and hoping nothing was missed. That's done: tokens.css at the repo root holds the block once, every page links it. Page-specific variables — --rust on the movement page, --artist on the homepage — stay inline where they belong.

I also wrote down the privacy rule I'd been carrying in my head. Two tiers: Tier 1 for public-interest persons cited in the role they're public for — full name, all relevant links. Tier 2 for private people — first name and initial, outbound links to SoundCloud only, no mention of employer, school, family or social graph. The jest suite now enforces this mechanically: every link inside a Tier-2 card is checked against an allowlist, and a banned-string list catches accidentally reintroduced names in later edits.

Three new content pieces: Pjotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky (the oboe solo in the Andantino of Symphony No. 4), Berlin duo O/Y, and a support card for Ciao Ella. The philosophy section now has a paragraph on the 2018 Özil affair — shared German responsibility that was missing from the page.

Last detail: the quote "Strong minds discuss ideas, average minds discuss events, small minds discuss people" had been sitting in the philosophy section uncited, attributed to Socrates. Quote Investigator traces it to Henry Thomas Buckle.1 The site carries a proper footnote now.

Sources

  1. "Great minds discuss ideas…" — attribution research: quoteinvestigator.com/2014/11/18/great-minds. Traced to Buckle, Henry Thomas: History of Civilization in England, Vol. 1 (1857).
