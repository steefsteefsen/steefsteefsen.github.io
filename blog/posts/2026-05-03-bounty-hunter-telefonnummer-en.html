Bounty: Who finds my number?

Here's a small game.

Whoever finds my phone number and calls me from the phone of a friend who already has my contact, gets a reward from me.

The same homepage. Personalised, tailored to you.

The condition is in the rule itself. You have to know someone in the tribe. Not me — someone who knows me and has my contact. You have to call from their phone. Not from a stranger's, not from a leaked one, not from an old address book dug up somewhere.

This is not a brute-force game. It's a tribe-pull game. The value isn't in finding a number — the value is in building a connection that someone else vouches for. That's exactly the logic of the tribe „direkt & heimlich" (direct & quiet): direct, because you shouldn't sneak it; quiet, because only the people who actually want to will reach out.

What you get

The same site architecture as mine: hero, pillar pages, blog, footnotes, i18n in 18 languages, privacy tier system, tests, MIT license. All open at theopenhomepage. But tailored to you. Your topics, your tribe, your idols, your sayings — we build a session together.

I don't charge money for this. What I get is the story: that someone understood the game. The personalisation is my form of thanks.

Why?

Because I see how the web default has become to find everything via search engines and platforms. Type a number, done. That's efficient, and it's also impoverished.

The opposite is not secrecy. The opposite is connection. I am findable — via the paths that already exist. Whoever uses the paths gets through. Whoever doesn't have the paths, builds them.

This is an invitation to build them.

If you call — say you read the bounty post. Otherwise I'll think you just want to chat. (Which is also fine, but then you get a conversation, not a homepage.)
