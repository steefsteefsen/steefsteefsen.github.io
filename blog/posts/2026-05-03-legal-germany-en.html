Legal Germany. The rest goes to the tribe.

This site speaks 18 languages. It says approximately the same thing in each — but „approximately" is exactly the problem under any one jurisdiction. What is harmless in Germany may look very different in Turkey, Russia, Iran, or China. What is opinion in Germany is, elsewhere, an indictment.

I built the site as if it speaks to all 18 language communities. But I cannot take legal responsibility for all 18 jurisdictions. I can barely do it for one.

What I take on

Legal Germany. I live here, I publish here, the Impressum entry is in Berlin (Baerwaldstr. 46). If the German content violates NetzDG, the German Criminal Code §§ 130/166/185, GDPR, copyright — that's my mistake, I take the consequence.

Concretely: I read every DE-language piece at least three times with the legal lens, check sources, ask people who know more than I do. I will make mistakes. I will correct them.

What I don't take on

The 17 other languages. Esperanto, English, Italian, French, Spanish, Portuguese, Danish, Ukrainian, Russian, Turkish, Hebrew, Arabic, Persian, Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Kurdish.

English I could just about manage, because UK/US/AU law is half-imaginable to me. But the others — no. If the Ukrainian translation is read in Russia, in a year where things tighten politically, I am not the right address for the consequences. People who read Russian know better what can happen. People who read Turkish know which words trigger. People who read Persian know the risk.

And a sketch that shows the flip side

Dave Chappelle had a series of sketches in the 2000s called „When Keeping It Real Goes Wrong". The point: someone decides to be maximally authentic in a situation, holds onto the self, takes the straight path — and it ends, every time, in a disaster that would have been avoidable had the person chosen the indirect, polite, tactful route.

Exactly the same risk lives in the plan to publish every language with the same clarity. „Keeping it real" across 18 jurisdictions is the guarantee that it goes wrong somewhere. Hence this call — not because I'm giving up, but because real in 18 languages means 18 different things.

Dave Chappelle — When Keeping It Real Goes Wrong. The point isn't „don't be real". The point is: being real has a context, and whoever ignores it pays.
