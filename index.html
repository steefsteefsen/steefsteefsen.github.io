Philosophy

What I Believe

I believe the best things grow slowly. Whether cultivating a garden, an idea, or a business — sustainable growth requires patience, deep roots, and an honest relationship with the soil you work in.

"He who plants a tree plants a hope."
— Lucy Larcom

My approach is shaped by the conviction that lasting value comes from understanding systems deeply, nurturing what matters, and building with integrity.

„Geld löst Verhaltensveränderungen aus und auf einmal gehen dich beschützende Männer um dein Haus."
— Retrogott
🌱

Cultivation

Treating every venture as a living system — feed it well, give it light, and have the patience to let it grow.

🪨

Sustainability

Building for decades, not quarters. Decisions rooted in long-term health over short-term extraction.

🌎

Connection

Everything is interconnected. The strongest systems are built through trust, transparency, and mutual benefit.

🤝

Inclusion

The table should be bigger. Good ideas come from everywhere — not just from the usual rooms. Building systems that make space for people who are usually left out.

In Development

A platform offering low-threshold support for people struggling with gambling, alcohol, nicotine, and other substances used in ways that harm themselves or the people around them. Currently looking for beta testers — I am one myself.

A funding campaign is in preparation to cover the formation costs (~€12,000), advised by WINHELLER Rechtsanwaltsgesellschaft mbH, Frankfurt am Main, who have proposed to support the company formation, data protection framework, and ongoing compliance. One of the intended funding sources is the Lotto-Stiftung Berlin.

Journey

Where I've Been

The path that shaped my thinking and brought me here.

Der Name Hüllinghorst kommt von einem Hof. Horst hat unter dem Meier auf dem Hof von Hülling gearbeitet — das ist, wo ich herkomme. Ich habe Häuser restauriert, im Garten gearbeitet, Menschen mit Behinderung gepflegt. Als Chefkoch in Kroatien, Italien und Frankreich. In einer Spedition, in einem Lager. Ich weiß, wie die Welt aussieht. Ich bin ein Macher — das hab ich mit Megaloh gemeinsam.

TU Berlin

Master of Science

I studied mostly among men, having grown up with three older sisters. The contrast was formative. What stayed was something more valuable than the degree: the ability to think scientifically, trust evidence, and question assumptions — including my own.

Three Years

Insolvency

Three years without money. It is not a chapter I am ashamed of — it is one I learned more from than almost anything else. Scarcity teaches you what actually matters and what you were just carrying out of habit.

Mondiali

Die Zwote & the Mondiali Antirazzisti

My team won the Mondiali Antirazzisti in my absence. I am genuinely proud of every person who made that happen. I am also still thinking critically about the role alcohol played in the money we raised for charity — some contradictions deserve to stay open.

Idols

People Who Shaped My Thinking

Not role models to copy — but minds that opened something.

🐧

Linus Torvalds

Built Linux out of stubbornness and precision. Proof that open, patient, unglamorous work changes the world more than any pitch deck.

✊

Die Jungs vom 36

After the Wall fell, Kreuzberg 36 was flooded with neo-nazis moving in from the East. The boys from SO36 didn't wait for the state. They cleared the streets themselves — systematically, block by block. Kreuzberg stayed Kreuzberg. Thank you.

🎤

Megaloh — der Malocher

German hip-hop with depth, craft, and integrity. He works. No shortcuts, no sellout. The music earns its place.

🎵

Mira — Berlin DJ

For what she builds in a room at night. Aware, precise, present. The kind of artist who knows that context is everything.

🎵

SSIO — Sohrab Saadat Ladjevardi

Persian-German rapper from Frankfurt. Born Sohrab Saadat Ladjevardi. The most honest voice in German hip-hop — no features, no compromises, no brand deals. He builds a world out of words, completely on his own terms.

💧

Neven Subotic

Fled a war as a child. Became a Bundesliga champion. Then donated 4 million euros of his own money to bring clean water to 450,000 people in East Africa — during his playing career, not after.1 Zero administrative overhead. He connects lived experience of displacement to structural action. That is rare. — I was a BVB fan until the club invested in Rheinmetall. After that it became just another club.

✨

Albert Einstein

Curiosity as a life philosophy. He questioned what everyone accepted and trusted the process of thinking over the comfort of certainty.

📚

Sokrates

Knew that knowing nothing is the beginning of wisdom. Never wrote a word — his questions did all the work.2

🏭

Platon

Took the questions of his teacher and built a system of thought that still holds weight 2,400 years later. Ideas outlast everything.3

👁

Edward Snowden

Gave up everything to tell the truth. Whatever you think of the consequences, the act of choosing conscience over comfort is something I respect deeply.4

🌎

Jimmy Wales

Founded Wikipedia — free knowledge for every human being on the planet. One of the most quietly radical acts in the history of the internet.5

☮

Dalai Lama

Compassion as a practice, not a feeling. The idea that inner peace and outer responsibility are not in conflict — that you can be both grounded and engaged.

📷

Sebastião Salgado

He photographs what the world would rather look away from — salt workers, gold miners, refugees — with a beauty that makes ignorance impossible. *The Salt of the Earth* is the most honest document of our time I have seen. And then he spent decades replanting the Atlantic Forest. He doesn't just witness. He acts.

🌈

Marina Abramović

She uses her own body as the material — pain, endurance, presence, trust. The work doesn't ask you to think about art. It asks you to feel what it means to be alive in a room with another person. That kind of courage in front of an audience is something I deeply respect.

🎤

Dave Chappelle

Walked away from $50 million at the height of his fame because something felt wrong.10 Came back years later — sharper, freer, on his own terms. The truth first. The money second. That order matters.

🎵

Wu-Tang Clan

Nine individuals — RZA, GZA, and the rest — who built something no one had built before, fell apart, and then came back together. Protect Ya Neck. C.R.E.A.M. Forever. A collective that proved that brotherhood, craft, and independence can survive time. Reunited — because some things are worth coming back to.

🎬

Jim Jarmusch

Slow cinema that refuses to explain itself. Characters at the margins, deadpan silences, no moral conclusions drawn for you. He trusts the audience. That trust is a form of respect that most filmmakers can't afford — or don't bother with.

🎧

Fatih Akın

Hamburg. Turkish-German. Between worlds and belonging fully to both. *Gegen die Wand* hit something real — rage, love, identity, the mess of it all. He makes films from where he actually stands, not from where it's comfortable to stand.

🎤

Leonard Cohen

Poet, novelist, songwriter. He wrote about love, loss, faith, and failure with a precision that made clichés impossible. *Hallelujah* took years to write and decades to be understood. He released his final album three weeks before he died. Some people work until the end — not out of compulsion, but because the work is the point.

🧠

Vera F. Birkenbihl

German author, speaker, and learning expert. She spent her life dismantling the idea that intelligence is fixed — arguing that the brain is a muscle, not a rank. She made complex ideas accessible without dumbing them down. That is harder than it looks, and rarer than it should be.
