# The Greater Good — What We're Working Toward

Read this before you write code. Re-read it when you forget why.

---

## The premise

Most software is built to extract. Attention, data, money, time. We're not doing that.

We build for people who are not the customer of the platforms they live inside. The user, not the user-as-product. That's the line.

## What "the greater good" means here

Three concentric circles. Work outward.

### 1. The person in front of you
Whoever uses what we ship — Timo's first translation, Stefan's blog, CareerGraph's matching — that one human is the test. If they leave better off than they arrived, we did the job. If they leave manipulated, addicted, surveilled, or confused, we failed, no matter how clean the code is.

### 2. The people they belong to
Communities, families, neighborhoods, scenes. The people Subotic's foundation feeds. The kids Werrepiraten coaches. The artists Rec & Play hosts. The ones the algorithm ignores. We build things that strengthen those bonds, not replace them with a feed.

### 3. The systems that hold them
Free knowledge (Wikipedia). Free money for the people who need it (Mein Grundeinkommen). Free water (Viva con Agua). Free models (LAION). Free protocols (the CCC's whole point). When the systems are healthy, the people are. When the systems rot — surveillance capitalism, attention markets, opaque AI — the people rot too.

## The non-negotiables

These are not preferences. They are the floor.

1. **Privacy is a right, not a feature.** No tracking we wouldn't want done to us. No dark patterns. No cookie walls.
2. **Open by default.** Open source, open data, open standards — unless there's a real reason not to, and "competitive advantage" isn't one.
3. **Honesty over polish.** A rough true thing beats a polished lie. If a project failed, say so. If a claim is unverified, footnote it.
4. **Fact-check before you ship.** Especially about people, software, money, history. Primary sources only. The world has enough confidently wrong content.
5. **No dunking.** Critique systems, not individuals. The open letters to Watzke and Musk argue with the position, not the person. Stay there.
6. **The work serves the user. Always.** Not the metric, not the funder, not the platform. If the user and the metric disagree, the metric is wrong.

## Who we learn from

The idol cards on the homepage are not decoration. They're the syllabus.

- **Socrates, Plato:** ask the question under the question.
- **Snowden:** the cost of telling the truth is real and worth paying.
- **Subotic:** use the platform you have for someone who has none.
- **Chappelle, SSIO:** the ones who refused the easy money. Note who they became.
- **Özil:** what happens when you speak up and the institution turns. Build for a world where that costs less.

## How this shows up in the work

- We translate into 17 languages because language is access, and access is dignity.
- We footnote because readers deserve receipts.
- We use plain HTML because every dependency is a future failure mode someone else has to debug.
- We test before we push because "it worked on my machine" is contempt for the next person.
- We mentor (this folder exists) because the work outlives any one of us.

## What we don't do

- We don't optimize for engagement. We optimize for whether you came back because we helped, not because we hooked you.
- We don't sell user data. Ever. Not to "trusted partners," not "anonymized," not at all.
- We don't ship features just because we can. Every feature is weight. Most ideas should die.
- We don't pretend AI is neutral. It's a tool with biases baked in by who trained it on what. Use it like that.

## The one-line version

**Build things that make the people who use them more free, not less.**

If a decision passes that test, ship it. If it doesn't, kill it.

That's the whole job.
