# ETN — First 30 Days

The point of this doc: ship something real before the energy fades. Nothing here is grand. All of it is concrete.

Today's date: **2026-04-25.** Deadline: **2026-05-25.**

---

## Week 1 (Apr 25 – May 1) — Stand it up

- [ ] **Pick a Matrix homeserver.** Three options:
  1. **matrix.org** — easiest, public, fine for now. You can migrate later.
  2. **A German/EU homeserver** — `tchncs.de`, `kde.org`, `envs.net`. Better aligned with values, slightly more friction.
  3. **Self-host** (Synapse or Conduit on your own VPS) — most control, real work, can wait until month 2.
  - **Recommendation:** start on matrix.org *or* tchncs.de this week. Don't self-host until there are 10+ people who'd notice if it broke.
- [ ] **Create the room.** Suggested name: `#european-tribe:<server>`. Make it invite-only. Encryption on by default.
- [ ] **Write the room description and pinned message.** Pin a link to the README and the philosophy doc.
- [ ] **Invite the first 5 people.** Real names you'd call. Not a list — actual humans you trust.

## Week 2 (May 2 – May 8) — Make it feel like a place

- [ ] **First conversation prompt.** Don't just have it sit empty. Stefan posts one thing the day after the first invites: a question, an observation, a link, a piece of music. Set the tone.
- [ ] **Add a brief code of conduct** (one paragraph, in the pinned message). Cribbed: "no dunking, no doomscroll dumping, no algorithm-bait. Disagree about ideas, not people. If you wouldn't say it in someone's kitchen, don't say it here."
- [ ] **Decide on subspaces or none.** Recommendation: none yet. One room. When there are 20 people and clear topic clusters, split.
- [ ] **Each new member writes a 3-sentence intro.** Required. No lurkers in the founding circle.

## Week 3 (May 9 – May 15) — Do one real thing together

- [ ] **Pick one thing to do as a group, not just talk about.** Examples:
  - A coordinated review of one EU policy proposal (Chat Control, AI Act enforcement, etc.) — each person reads a section, shares notes.
  - A shared recommendation list: 10 books / 10 albums / 10 tools each member will not shut up about.
  - A "where we are" map: each person drops a city pin, so people can find IRL neighbors.
  - A single open letter the room signs.
- [ ] **Stefan picks one and starts it.** Don't put it to a vote — momentum matters more than democracy at week 3.

## Week 4 (May 16 – May 25) — Decide what month 2 looks like

- [ ] **Honest review.** Stefan posts: who showed up, what worked, what didn't, what to kill.
- [ ] **Decide on month 2's one thing.** Examples:
  - First in-person meetup (NRW, since that's where Stefan and likely most of round one are).
  - Spin up the Nextcloud / shared file space.
  - Add the kids track (separate room, parents-and-kids, age-appropriate).
  - Open the Matrix room to friends-of-members (still invite-only, one ring out).
- [ ] **Update the README** with what was learned. Cut the bits that turned out to be wrong.

---

## What we measure

Nothing automated. Once a month, Stefan asks:

1. Did anyone leave better off than they arrived?
2. Did anyone do something IRL because of this room?
3. Did the noise stay out?

If yes/yes/yes — keep going. If no — fix it or close it. Don't keep a thing alive out of pride.

## What we don't measure

- Member count.
- Message count.
- Engagement.

We are not building an audience. We are gathering a tribe.
