# European Tribe Network (ETN)

A network of people in Europe who refuse the spectacle and build the alternative.

---

## Why

The loudest voices online are not the wisest. The platforms that amplify them are not neutral. The political theater — Trump, Epstein, Musk, the daily outrage cycle — is not news, it is a product, and we are not its audience.

We are tired of being told that paying attention to noise is the same as caring about the world.

There is another way to live online and offline: in real groups of real people, with tools we control, talking about what actually matters where we actually live.

That's what this is.

## What it is

- A network. Not an app, not a brand, not a movement-with-merch.
- People across Europe who share the values: privacy as a right, openness by default, honesty over polish, community over feed.
- Tools we control: Matrix for chat, Bitwarden EU for secrets, Ente for 2FA, Nextcloud for files, our own domains for everything public.
- Local nodes (a person, a city, a project) loosely connected. No HQ.
- Open to kids. They are not future members. They are members.

## What it is not

- Not a political party.
- Not an anti-American thing. Not anti-anyone. Anti-spectacle.
- Not a closed club. But also not a free-for-all — you have to actually share the values, not just like the aesthetic.
- Not a place to argue about Trump or Putin or Musk or whoever the algorithm wants you angry about today. There are 10,000 places for that. This is not one.

## The values (short version)

From the philosophy doc — re-read it.

1. The person in front of you is the test.
2. Strengthen the bonds, don't replace them with a feed.
3. Keep the systems healthy: free knowledge, free water, free protocols.
4. Privacy is a right. Open by default. Honesty over polish. Fact-check. No dunking. The work serves the user.

If those land, you're already in. If they don't, that's fine — go in peace.

## How to join (today)

1. You know Stefan. Or someone Stefan knows.
2. You join the Matrix room (link by invite).
3. You introduce yourself: name, where you live, what you actually do, what you'd want to bring.
4. You read the philosophy doc and this one. If anything makes you flinch, say so — better now than later.

## What we're not going to do

- We're not going to build an app for this.
- We're not going to make a Discord. Matrix or nothing.
- We're not going to chase numbers. 30 real people > 3000 lurkers.
- We're not going to perform our values for an audience. We just live them.
